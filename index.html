Constellations

A constellation is a group of stars that form a recognizable pattern in the night sky. These patterns often resemble objects, animals, or mythological figures, and they have been used for centuries by various civilizations for navigation, storytelling, and as a way to understand the cosmos. Astronomers divide the sky into 88 official constellations, covering the entire celestial sphere. Each constellation holds a unique position in the sky and serves as a roadmap for identifying stars, planets, and deep-sky objects such as galaxies and nebulae.

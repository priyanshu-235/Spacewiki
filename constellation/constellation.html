Constellations

  • Orion
  • Ursa Major
  • Cassiopeia
  • Lyra

Orion

Orion, also known as the Hunter, is one of the most recognizable constellations in the night sky. It features prominent stars such as Betelgeuse and Rigel. The Orion Nebula, located in this constellation, is a stunning star-forming region.

Ursa Major

Ursa Major, or the Great Bear, is home to the Big Dipper, a well-known asterism. This constellation has been used for navigation for centuries and is visible year-round in the northern hemisphere.

Cassiopeia

Cassiopeia is a W-shaped constellation located in the northern sky. It is named after the vain queen of Greek mythology and contains several beautiful deep-sky objects like the Heart Nebula and the Pacman Nebula.

Lyra

Lyra, the Lyre, is a small constellation with a rich history in Greek mythology. It contains Vega, one of the brightest stars in the sky, and the famous Ring Nebula, a stunning planetary nebula.

Milky Way

The Milky Way is our home galaxy, containing billions of stars, planets, and other celestial bodies. It spans 100,000 light-years across and features a bright core, surrounded by spiral arms.

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy is the nearest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way, located 2.5 million light-years away. It is on a collision course with our galaxy in billions of years.

Whirlpool Galaxy

The Whirlpool Galaxy is famous for its clearly defined spiral arms and is located about 23 million light-years from Earth. It is interacting with a smaller galaxy, NGC 5195.

Sombrero Galaxy

The Sombrero Galaxy is known for its bright nucleus and large central bulge, surrounded by a vivid dust ring, giving it the appearance of a sombrero hat.

Black Eye Galaxy

The Black Eye Galaxy, also known as the Sleeping Beauty Galaxy, is famous for its dark dust lane, which gives it a distinct, 'black eye' appearance.
